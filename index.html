Development in Astronomy

Solar System Models

The study of astronomy has evolved significantly over centuries, with different models proposed to explain our solar system.

Geocentric Model

Proposed by Ptolemy (90-168 AD)

Greek astronomer, astrologer and geographer

Earth at the center of the solar system with circular orbits

"Geo" means Earth, "centric" means center

Earth is stationary and all objects revolve around it

Heliocentric Model

Proposed by Copernicus (1473-1543)

Polish astronomer, mathematician, economist and doctor

Sun at the center of the solar system with circular orbits

"Helio" means Sun, "centric" means center

Earth rotates on its axis and revolves around the Sun

Modified Heliocentric Model

Proposed by Kepler (1571-1630)

German astronomer, mathematician and astrologer

Sun at one common focal point with planets following elliptical orbits according to Kepler's Law

Rocket Technology

How Rockets Work

Rocket Principle

Rockets are used widely in space explorations. When the fuel in a rocket burns, hot gases are released at high speed through the bottom of the rocket. The release of these gases produces a force which pushes the rocket upwards.

Rocket Development

Rockets have evolved from simple fireworks to complex spacecraft:

  • 11th century: Chinese invented gunpowder and used primitive rockets in battles
  • 20th century: Modern rockets developed for space exploration
  • Saturn V: Rocket used to send astronauts to the Moon

Rocket Types

Various rockets have been developed for different purposes:

  • Launch vehicles: Saturn V, Falcon 9, Soyuz
  • Space shuttles: Reusable spacecraft
  • Interplanetary rockets: For missions to other planets

Rocket Question

Based on Photograph 10.1, which rocket was used to send astronauts to the Moon?

Answer: Saturn V rocket was used for Apollo Moon missions

Remote Sensing Technology

Remote Sensing Applications

What is Remote Sensing?

Remote sensing is a method of gathering and recording information from a distance.

Malaysia's Remote Sensing

In Malaysia, remote sensing instruments are fitted to TiungSAT-1 to receive or detect visible, ultraviolet and infrared lights produced by objects on the surface or below the surface of Earth.

The information gathered by TiungSAT-1 is sent to two data receiving stations:

  • National Planetarium Station, Federal Territory of Kuala Lumpur
  • Mission Control Station (MCCB), Bangi, Selangor

Importance of Space Technology

Benefits of Space Exploration

Scientific Knowledge

Space exploration advances our understanding of the universe, from the formation of galaxies to the possibility of life on other planets.

Technological Innovation

Space technology drives innovation that benefits life on Earth:

  • Satellite communications
  • GPS navigation systems
  • Weather forecasting
  • Earth monitoring for climate change

International Cooperation

Space exploration promotes international collaboration, such as the International Space Station (ISS) involving multiple countries.

Economic Benefits

Space technology creates new industries and job opportunities:

  • Satellite communications industry
  • Space tourism development
  • Asteroid mining possibilities
  • Technology spin-offs for everyday use

Inspiration for STEM

Space exploration inspires new generations to pursue education and careers in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM).

Malaysia's Space Program

Malaysia has developed its space capabilities through:

  • TiungSAT-1 microsatellite program
  • MEASAT communication satellites
  • National Space Agency (Agensi Angkasa Negara)
  • Participation in international space research
