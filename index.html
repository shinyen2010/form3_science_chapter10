Development in Astronomy

Solar System Models

The study of astronomy has evolved significantly over centuries, with different models proposed to explain our solar system.

Geocentric Model

Proposed by Ptolemy (90-168 AD)

Greek astronomer, astrologer and geographer

Earth at the center of the solar system with circular orbits

"Geo" means Earth, "centric" means center

Earth is stationary and all objects revolve around it

Heliocentric Model

Proposed by Copernicus (1473-1543)

Polish astronomer, mathematician, economist and doctor

Sun at the center of the solar system with circular orbits

"Helio" means Sun, "centric" means center

Earth rotates on its axis and revolves around the Sun

Modified Heliocentric Model

Proposed by Kepler (1571-1630)

German astronomer, mathematician and astrologer

Sun at one common focal point with planets following elliptical orbits according to Kepler's Law

Space Technology Applications

Technologies for Space Exploration

Various technologies have been developed for space exploration with important applications:

Space Telescopes

Hubble Space Telescope placed in orbit 600 km from Earth

Spitzer space telescope observes distant activities in space

Radio telescopes detect radio waves from space

Rockets

Propelled by Newton's Third Law

Burning fuel releases hot gases, producing upward thrust

Saturn V rocket used for Apollo Moon missions

Satellites

First satellite Sputnik 1 launched in 1957

Thousands now orbit Earth for various purposes

Used for communication, weather, and research

Space Probes

Unmanned spacecraft that gather information

Travel beyond Earth's orbit into solar system

Carry cameras, sensors, and communication equipment

Remote Sensing Technology

Remote Sensing Applications

Remote sensing involves gathering information from a distance using satellites and other technology:

  • Malaysia's TungSAT-1 detects visible, ultraviolet, and infrared light
  • Data is sent to receiving stations at National Planetarium and Bangi, Selangor
  • Used in agriculture to detect suitable regions for development
  • Applied in geology to locate mineral sources and monitor land changes
  • Essential for disaster management to identify pollution and forest fires
  • Important for defense to detect intrusions

Space Probe Cassini

In 2017, space probe Cassini was still active orbiting Saturn even after 20 years in space.

Importance of Space Technology

Benefits of Space Exploration

Space exploration technology has numerous benefits:

  • Advances scientific knowledge and understanding of our universe
  • Drives technological innovation with practical Earth applications
  • Enables global communication through satellite networks
  • Improves weather forecasting and climate monitoring
  • Enhances national security and defense capabilities
  • Inspires new generations to pursue STEM education and careers

Malaysia's Role in Space Technology

Malaysia has developed its space capabilities through:

  • TiungSAT-1 microsatellite program
  • MEASAT communication satellites
  • National Space Agency (Agensi Angkasa Negara)
  • Participation in international space research
